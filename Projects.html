Student Project with Masoumeh Mansouri

My main research interest is hybrid planning that is to integrate automated task and motion planning, scheduling, as well as temporal and spatial reasoning. Currently, I mostly focus on the application of hybrid planning in multi-robot systems. I am also interested in the ethical and social implications of technology in general and robotics/AI in particular; as well as topics at the intersection of feminism and robotics.

Human-aware planning

Understanding the environment is a key requirement for any autonomous robot operation. The following set of projects concerns the environments in which both robots and humans are present, and human movements have an impact on the robot tasks. In general, incorporating human movement patterns in robot navigation will lead to safer and socially more acceptable robot trajectories.

  • Human-aware multi-robot task allocation: this project will develop a novel auction-based multi-robot task allocation algorithm. This algorithm takes into account human motion pattern as well as kino-dynamic motion constraints when distributing tasks to the robots in complex environments.
  • Human-aware multi-robot motion planner: this project will develop a multi-robot motion planner in which robot trajectories should avoid human trajectories as much as possible. This planner will be particularly useful for using robots in a warehouse environment, in which both humans and robots share the same space, and safety is a key factor. This algorithm takes into account human motion patterns as well as kino-dynamic motion constraints to plan feasible trajectories for each individual robots.

Requires: strong programming skills; knowledge of motion planning algorithms.
